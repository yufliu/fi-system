8 Recommendations & things to think about

Generated live from your realistic scenario under the active strategy — plus evergreen considerations.

    Things to think about

    • Taxes & entity: rental and management income are taxable — depreciation, cost segregation, and an LLC/holding structure can materially change your real spendable number. Loop in a CPA.
    • Cash reserves: hold 3–6 months of PITI per door plus a CapEx fund before accelerating payoffs — STR/MTR income is lumpier than LTR.
    • Interest-rate & refinance risk: rate drives per-door cash flow more than any other lever (see the sensitivity table) — refinance when it makes sense.
    • STR/MTR regulation & insurance: Tallahassee/FL short-term-rental rules, occupancy seasonality, and hurricane insurance can flip the STR-vs-LTR math — revisit the toggle yearly.
    • Liquidity & diversification: paying everything off maximizes cash flow but concentrates wealth in illiquid real estate — consider keeping some leverage or liquid assets.
    • Appreciation & exit: this model ignores appreciation (conservative) — real equity/net worth is likely higher; plan refis or 1031 exchanges as part of the path.

    ⚠️ Model assumptions & caveats

    • All figures are pre-tax. Your real spendable income is lower after income tax on rental profit and management income.
    • No appreciation is modeled — your real net worth is understated. "Equity" here = down payments + principal paid down only (excludes your existing properties' base equity and any market appreciation).
    • Rent growth defaults to 0% (rents held flat = conservative timeline). Set it above to model raises.
    • The plan assumes you reinvest all rental cash flow plus your annual contribution into acquisitions, then payoffs.
    • Snowball payoff applies extra cash to the smallest loan balance first; existing-property balances are estimates that affect payoff order. Existing loans are amortized at the template rate as a proxy.
    • Paid-off net for existing properties adds back the entire "Mortgage" line; if it includes escrowed tax/insurance, move those into "Misc" for accuracy.
